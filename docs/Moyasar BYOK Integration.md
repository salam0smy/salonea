# Salona Payment Architecture Plan: Moyasar BYOK Integration

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the payment infrastructure for **Salona**, a multi-tenant SaaS platform for salons. To ensure full compliance with Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) regulations and to eliminate financial liability for the platform, Salona will utilize a **"Bring Your Own Key" (BYOK)** architecture with Moyasar.

In this model, Salona acts strictly as a software provider, routing payments directly from the customer to the individual salon's bank account.

---

## 1. The BYOK (Bring Your Own Key) Strategy

Instead of Salona acting as a middleman collecting all funds and distributing payouts, each salon will operate its own independent Moyasar merchant account.

**Key Benefits:**

* **Zero Regulatory Friction:** Salona does not hold third-party funds, bypassing the need for complex payment aggregator licenses.
* **Instant Payouts:** Salons receive their money directly on Moyasar’s settlement schedule without waiting on Salona.
* **Simplified Accounting:** Refunds, chargebacks, and transaction fees are handled directly between the salon and Moyasar.

---

## 2. Key Management & Security

To facilitate payments on behalf of the salons, Salona needs to store specific credentials provided by Moyasar. Security is the top priority here.

The engineering team will need to manage three distinct keys for each salon:

* **Publishable Key:** * *Purpose:* Used on the Salona customer-facing booking page to safely load the Moyasar payment form.
* *Storage:* Stored as standard text in the database.


* **Secret Key:** * *Purpose:* Used strictly behind the scenes by the Salona server to verify transactions and automate setup.
* *Storage:* **Must be heavily encrypted** in the database. It should never be exposed to the public or the frontend application.


* **Webhook Secret:** * *Purpose:* A special password generated by Moyasar to prove that incoming payment notifications are authentic.
* *Storage:* Stored securely alongside the encrypted Secret Key.



---

## 3. The "Automated Webhook" Onboarding Flow

We want the onboarding experience for a new salon owner to be as frictionless as possible. They should not have to configure complex developer settings in Moyasar.

**The Desired User Experience:**

1. The salon owner creates a Moyasar account and copies their `Secret Key` and `Publishable Key`.
2. They paste these two keys into their Salona Admin Dashboard and click "Connect Moyasar."
3. **Behind the scenes (The Automation):** Salona's system immediately uses the Secret Key to contact Moyasar and automatically creates a "Webhook." It tells Moyasar: *"Whenever this salon gets a successful payment, send an alert to Salona's master server."*
4. Moyasar replies with a `Webhook Secret`, which Salona silently saves.

The salon owner sees a "Successfully Connected!" message in seconds, without doing any manual configuration.

---

## 4. The Transaction Lifecycle

When a customer books an appointment at "Salon A," the flow operates as follows:

**Phase 1: The Checkout**

* The customer selects a service and proceeds to checkout.
* Salona retrieves Salon A's `Publishable Key` to display the payment screen.
* The system attaches "invisible" metadata to the payment request, specifically noting the `Salon ID` and the `Appointment ID`.
* The customer enters their card details and pays.

**Phase 2: The Verification (The Webhook)**

* Moyasar successfully processes the card.
* Moyasar instantly sends a digital receipt (a webhook) to Salona's master server.
* Salona's server reads the incoming message, looks at the invisible metadata, and identifies that this payment belongs to `Salon ID` and `Appointment ID`.
* Salona checks the signature using Salon A's `Webhook Secret` to ensure the message isn't a fake.
* Once verified, Salona updates the appointment status in the database from "Pending" to "Paid."
